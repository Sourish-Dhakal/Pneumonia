The Respiratory System

The Respiratory System

A short description

The Respiratory System is a organ system in the human body. The Respiratory System is used to deliver oxygen to all the different parts of the body. It takes oxygen from the nose and goes to the lungs, where the blood cells arrive and take the oxygen. The blood in the circulatory system gets its oxygen from the respiratory system. The lungs are the place where gas exchange takes place. In the lungs, the oxygen goes in the blood cells, and the carbon dioxide comes from the blood cells. After the gas exchange, the carbon dioxide goes out, and more oxygen comes in. The respiratory system is important because it makes sure your body gets oxygen, which your organs need to work. The respiratory system allows you to talk and smell. It warms and moisturizes air to the level it needs. Protects your nose and mouth from dangerous stuff.

How does it work when it's healthy?

It takes in oxygen using the mouth and nose. (Inhaling) It goes down the throat and trachea where it reaches the lungs. In the lungs, it takes the carbon dioxide from the blood cells, and replaces it with oxygen. Then it goes to the circulatory system where the blood cells change the oxygen from carbon dioxide. Then the lungs take the carbon dioxide from the blood cells and bring them back up the same way the oxygen comes down and goes back out the nose. (Exhaling)

Organs

Mouth and Nose

Where you breath from. Oxygen comes in when you inhale and Carbon Dioxide goes out when you exhale.

Sinuses

Places between the bones in your body where air exists to control your body humidity and temperature.

Throat

The throat is a tube between your mouth and your trachea.

Trachea

The trachea is the windpipe and it connects your throat and lung.

Bronchial Tubes

Part in the lungs that connects the windpipe and the two lungs.

Lungs

Place that holds all the small places where gas exchange occurs.
