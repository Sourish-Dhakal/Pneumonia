Pronounced: nuh - mow - nyuh
Respiratory System

Pneumonia

A short description

Pneumonia is a disease. When your lungs get infected from some other illness, your immune system attacks the illness. Sometimes, it attacks your lungs. It's like skin, if you accidently cut yourself with a knife, you bleed. Same thing happens with Pneumonia, the blood is fluids/pus, the knife is your immune system and your skin are your lungs. To defend yourself, the 'knife' will attack the illness. But sometimes, it hits the lungs which causes Pneumonia. As it shows on the diagram on the left of me, Pneumonia creates a liquid in the Alveoli of the lungs. Sometimes the liquid is pus.

Illnesses that can cause Pneumonia include:

  • Common Cold
  • COVID-19
  • Flu
  • Human Metapneumovirus
  • Human Parainfluenza Virus
  • Legionnaire's disease
  • Mycoplasma Pneumonia Bacteria
  • Pneumococcal disease
  • Pneumocystis Pneumonia
  • Respiratory Syncytial Virus

Fun Facts!

Pneumonia

Fun Facts

  1. Bacterial Pneumonia is worse and more severe than Viral Pneumonia.
  2. Pneumonia in both lungs is called double Pneumonia.
  3. It's easier to get Pneumonia if:
    • You're over 65 years or under 2 years of age
    • Have a lung or heart condition.
      • Cystic Fibrosis
      • Asthma
      • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease
      • Emphysema
      • Pulmonary Fibrosis
      • Sarcoidosis
    • You have a neurological condition that makes swallowing harder.
      • Dementia
      • Parkinson's Disease
      • Stroke
    • You are in the hospital or a long-term healthcare facility.
    • You smoke.
    • You're pregnant.
    • Have a weakened immune system
      • You're on Chemotherapy
      • Had Organ Transplant
      • Have HIV / AIDS
      • Taking medications that suppress your immune system.
  4. Adults are more likely to get bacterial pneumonia and kids are more likely to get viral pneumonia.
  5. Pneumonia is life-threatening, but it can get better in two weeks.
